StereoisomersÂ¶

Considering each atom having a unique name and index within a residue, n stereo centers results in 2n unique stereoisomers. In generating an initial liquid mixture, the number of monomers of a certain type requested is distributed as uniformly as possible among all its stereoisomers (i.e., a racemic mixture is generated by default). HTPolyNet builds these stereoisomer *.gro files automatically, and they are named MONO-SC-0-0-...-0.gro, where MONO is the original molecule name, -SC- indicates sterocenters, and the chain of 0 and 1 separated by dashes indicate the chirality of each stereocenter. 0 just refers to the handedness the atom has in the original input *.mol2 file, and 1 refers to the opposite handedness.
